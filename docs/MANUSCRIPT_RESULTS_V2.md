# Manuscript Results v2

## Results

### Attraction–defence regimes were conditional across the declared theoretical envelope

The route-based model did not mechanically force either complementarity or
substitutability between attraction and defence/access investment. Across 162 local
phenotype–interaction cases, four biological parameter scenarios, and four
predeclared functional-form variants, we evaluated 2,592 local A×D mixed partials.
Of these, 1,279 (49.3%) were positive and 1,313 (50.7%) were negative. The overall
split itself is not interpreted as an empirical frequency. Rather, it shows that both
regimes occupy substantial parts of the declared model space and that neither sign is
a built-in consequence of the model.

Regime sign changed in the expected direction with the balance of route-specific
benefits and costs. Under high attraction tracking, low pollination obstruction, and
low shared cost, 597 of 648 evaluations (92.1%) were complementary. Under high
pollination obstruction and high shared cost, only 42 of 648 evaluations (6.5%) were
complementary and 606 (93.5%) were substitutable. The baseline parameter scenario was
close to evenly divided, with 315 complementary and 333 substitutable evaluations.
Thus, the model predicts sign switching when the antagonist-mediated benefit of
combining attraction and defence is outweighed by pollination obstruction and shared
costs, rather than a universal attraction–defence relationship.

The interaction environment shifted the sign in the same mechanistic direction.
Pooling only across the predeclared phenotype points, parameter scenarios, and
functional-form variants, the complementary fraction increased with floral damage
pressure at each level of pollinator service. At pollinator service 0.2, for example,
the complementary fraction increased from 36.8% at floral damage pressure 0.2 to
77.4% at floral damage pressure 0.8. In contrast, at floral damage pressure 0.2, the
complementary fraction declined from 36.8% at pollinator service 0.2 to 16.7% at
pollinator service 0.8. These sign frequencies are theoretical summaries over the
declared grid, not empirical probabilities, but they make the model's regime logic
explicit: stronger antagonist-mediated benefit favours complementarity, whereas
stronger pollination opportunity makes defence-related obstruction more costly.

### The conditional-regime result was not restricted to one functional form

We next separated biological parameter sensitivity from functional-form sensitivity.
The baseline, saturating-attraction, saturating-defence, and joint
saturation-plus-curved-cost variants changed the prevalence and location of positive
and negative mixed partials, as expected. Saturating attraction produced 69.0%
complementary evaluations, whereas the joint saturating-both, curved-shared-cost form
produced 32.1% complementary evaluations. However, within a fixed biological
parameter scenario, 395 of 648 local case × scenario combinations (61.0%) retained
the same non-neutral sign across all four predeclared functional forms.

No local case retained one sign across the entire biological-parameter envelope. This
is not evidence against robustness of the core conditional claim, because changes in
tracking, pollination obstruction, defence efficacy, and shared cost are the proposed
mechanisms of regime switching. The relevant theoretical result is therefore the
combination of two findings: sign boundaries move under alternative response curves,
but a majority of local case × parameter-scenario combinations retain their sign
across all four functional forms, while changing the biological route balance can
still switch the regime.

### The *Impatiens* archive provided a linked empirical case study of a partial route structure

The title-validated *Impatiens capensis* archive provided a linked single-system panel
containing early flower redness, early floral condensed tannins, pollinator use,
natural floral damage, reproductive components, flowering phenology, and randomized
supplemental robbing, florivory, and pollination treatments. We used this system as an
empirical case study of route components rather than as a calibration dataset for the
full theoretical model.

The response-scale channel models used archived measurement units. Pollinator use was
analysed as a 60-minute-standardized visit count/rate. Natural floral damage was
analysed as an individual-flower tissue-loss fraction with plant-cluster robust
inference. Seeds per chasmogamous fruit were analysed as individual-fruit seed counts,
again with plant-cluster robust inference. Plant-level predictors were standardized
once per plant before expansion to repeated flower or fruit records.

### A floral-tannin candidate was associated with lower pollinator use, but not with resolved protection from natural floral damage

A one-standard-deviation increase in log1p early floral condensed tannins was
associated with a lower 60-minute-standardized pollinator-use rate (RR = 0.682, 95%
CI 0.479–0.972). Because floral tannins were not experimentally manipulated, this
coefficient is interpreted as a pollination-cost-like observational association, not
as a causal tannin effect.

The same tannin candidate was not resolved as reducing natural floral tissue loss in
the flower-level fractional-logit model (OR = 1.121, 95% CI 0.990–1.269), and the
point estimate was not in the predicted protective direction. The case study therefore
showed an asymmetric route pattern: a pollination-cost-like association was visible,
whereas the corresponding antagonism-suppression benefit was not resolved.

Flower redness was not resolved as a predictor of either pollinator use (RR = 0.981,
95% CI 0.626–1.539) or natural floral damage (OR = 1.064, 95% CI 0.933–1.213). These
results do not imply that floral attraction is biologically irrelevant; they indicate
that the declared archived panel did not isolate a precise flower-redness route to
either response.

### Zero-heavy pollinator observations concentrated the tannin association in positive visit intensity

Among the 81 complete plants in the primary pollinator-rate model, 52 had zero
observed visits, and the Pearson dispersion statistic was 23.18. A declared
post-primary hurdle sensitivity therefore separated visit occurrence from positive
visit intensity. The tannin candidate was not resolved as a predictor of whether any
visit occurred (OR = 0.780, 95% CI 0.511–1.191). Among plants with at least one
observed visit, however, the tannin candidate was associated with lower positive visit
intensity (RR = 0.515, 95% CI 0.332–0.799). This sensitivity is interpreted as a model-
adequacy diagnostic and not as a replacement for the primary pollinator-rate model.

### The A × D candidate seed surface was unresolved

The association between early flower redness and the floral-tannin candidate did not
resolve an interaction for seeds per chasmogamous fruit (RR = 1.008, 95% CI
0.968–1.049). This reproductive-component analysis does not estimate total fitness,
the Part A mixed partial, or a shared allocation-cost parameter. It instead shows that
the archived seed-count data do not reveal a strong A × D candidate surface under the
declared model.

### Randomized imposed florivory reduced chasmogamous fruit production

The randomized Robbing × Florivory × Pollination design contained 200 plants in valid
assignment cells, with exactly 25 plants in each of the eight treatment combinations.
The descriptive baseline-complete audit retained 170 plants, with 19–24 plants per
cell. In the full effect-coded 2 × 2 × 2 factorial model, retaining all two-way and
three-way treatment interactions and adjusting for pre-treatment phenology, assigned
supplemental florivory reduced chasmogamous fruits per plant per day (standardized
coefficient = -0.290, 95% CI -0.540 to -0.040). This is the clearest causal result in
the empirical case study: experimentally imposed floral damage reduced a reproductive
component.

The same florivory assignment did not resolve an effect on seeds per chasmogamous
fruit (standardized coefficient = -0.142, 95% CI -0.622 to 0.338). Thus, the detected
reproductive cost of imposed florivory was expressed through fruit production rather
than through a resolved change in seed number per chasmogamous fruit.

### Phenology was a major axis of reproductive variation

Later first chasmogamous flowering was associated with lower chasmogamous fruits per
plant per day (standardized coefficient = -0.493, 95% CI -0.617 to -0.369). This
association was stronger and more precise than any flower-redness route in the
response-scale models, indicating that seasonal timing is a major ecological axis in
this system and should remain explicit in future attraction–defence tests.

### The empirical case constrained, but did not calibrate, the theoretical regime structure

Taken together, the theoretical and empirical results support different levels of
inference. The theoretical sweep shows that attraction–defence complementarity and
substitutability can emerge from different balances among antagonist-mediated benefit,
pollination obstruction, and shared cost, and that these sign changes are not limited
to a single functional form. The *Impatiens* case study then identifies only a subset
of those route components in one biological system: a pollination-cost-like
association for a floral chemistry candidate, no resolved protective association for
that candidate, and an experimental reproductive cost of imposed florivory.

The *Impatiens* data therefore do not directly establish complementarity or
substitutability and do not estimate the theoretical mixed partial. Their role is to
anchor the route framework in a linked empirical system and to demonstrate an
asymmetric case in which one cost-like route is visible while the corresponding
protective route remains unresolved.

### Literature synthesis was retained only as route-level pattern support

The literature synthesis was not used to estimate universal causal parameters for the
full framework. Instead, it was retained as supporting evidence for whether route-like
patterns recur across systems. The clearest currently verified cross-study pattern is
a `c_D`-like direction in which some chemical defence/access traits are associated
with reduced pollinator use. Other routes remain too heterogeneous for a single pooled
parameter under the current compatibility rules. This limitation is treated as a
boundary on generalisation rather than as the main result of the manuscript.

## Claims retained for Discussion

```text
Supported theoretically:
  - attraction-defence sign is conditional on route balance rather than universal;
  - higher antagonist-mediated benefit shifts the declared grid toward complementarity;
  - higher pollination obstruction/shared cost shifts the declared grid toward substitutability;
  - the conditional-regime result persists across predeclared functional-form variants,
    although exact boundaries remain assumption-sensitive.

Supported empirically in the Impatiens case:
  - D candidate -> pollinator-use cost-like observational association;
  - imposed florivory -> reduced CH fruits/day causal assignment contrast;
  - phenology -> strong reproductive-component association.

Unresolved empirically:
  - A -> pollinator use;
  - A -> natural floral damage;
  - D candidate -> natural floral-damage suppression;
  - A × D candidate -> CH seed component.

Not identified:
  - causal effects of flower redness or floral tannins;
  - total lifetime fitness surface;
  - empirical Part A mixed partial;
  - c_AD calibration;
  - direct empirical proof of complementarity or substitutability.
```
