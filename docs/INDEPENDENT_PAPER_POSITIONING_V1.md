# Independent paper positioning v1

## Decision

The `bita` project should be written as an independent manuscript, not merely as a
supporting theory engine for a later *Campanula* or island-flower paper.

```text
chosen path: A — independent paper
paper type:  conditional theory + empirical case study
supporting role: literature evidence map as context and claim boundary
not chosen:  bita only as a background framework for a separate focal-system paper
```

## Why this can stand alone

The manuscript has a coherent two-part contribution, with the literature map kept as
supporting context rather than a main novelty claim:

```text
1. A formal conditional framework for attraction-defence/access trait regimes.
2. A linked Impatiens capensis case study showing asymmetric empirical support:
   a pollination-cost-like chemistry association and an experimental cost of imposed
   florivory, but no resolved protective benefit or A × D component surface.
```

The novelty is not that plant defence has costs, that herbivory lowers reproduction,
or that pollinators respond to floral traits. Those are established ideas. The
novelty is the explicit route-based logic for when floral attraction and
floral defence/access traits should be complementary, substitutable, or unresolved,
plus a disciplined single-system application that shows how the framework constrains
interpretation when only some routes are empirically visible.

## Core story

The paper should tell this story:

```text
Floral attraction and floral defence/access traits should not be expected to show a
universal trade-off or a universal positive association. Their relationship depends
on the balance among attraction-mediated pollination gains, attraction-mediated
antagonist exposure, defence/access-mediated antagonist suppression,
defence/access-mediated pollination costs, and shared A × D costs.
```

The empirical evidence is deliberately partial:

```text
- Impatiens: floral tannin candidate is associated with reduced pollinator use.
- Impatiens: same tannin candidate is not resolved as protective against natural
  floral damage.
- Impatiens: imposed florivory experimentally reduces CH fruit production.
- Literature map: retained only as context showing that broad cross-study evidence
  is not strong enough to replace a careful case-study interpretation.
```

This is a constraint on the theory, not a proof of the complete regime.

## Main claim

A safe main claim is:

> We provide a conditional framework for floral attraction-defence/access regimes and
> apply it to a linked Impatiens case study. The case study shows a floral chemistry
> candidate with a pollination-cost-like association but no resolved protective
> benefit, while imposed florivory reduces a reproductive component.

## What makes the paper publishable

The manuscript is publishable in scope because it does two things that are useful
even without proving complementarity/substitutability directly:

```text
1. It prevents an overly simple attraction-vs-defence story by deriving conditional
   route logic.
2. It gives a concrete empirical case showing how the framework behaves when applied
   to linked data with both observational trait channels and randomized downstream
   damage treatments.
```

This is strongest as a conceptual ecology / methods-informed empirical case-study
paper, not as a high-claim general meta-analysis. The literature map can remain in
the Methods/Supplement or a short background subsection, but it should not be sold as
the main new result.

## What would weaken the paper

The paper becomes weak if it claims any of the following:

```text
- floral tannins causally reduce pollinator visitation;
- floral tannins are proven defensive traits in the Impatiens panel;
- attraction-defence complementarity or substitutability was directly observed;
- the Impatiens A × D coefficient is the Part A mixed partial;
- c_AD was estimated;
- existing literature supports universal pooled route parameters;
- the inability to estimate universal four-route parameters is itself the main
  contribution.
```

These claims should remain forbidden.

## Role of Campanula and island systems

*Campanula*, island systems, and pollinator-loss hypotheses should be mentioned only
as future applications or motivation, not as evidence in this manuscript.

Safe wording:

```text
The framework can guide future studies of island floral evolution, pollinator loss,
and nectar-guide reduction by specifying which route-specific quantities must be
measured jointly.
```

Unsafe wording:

```text
The Impatiens case demonstrates the mechanism behind island Campanula nectar-guide
loss.
```

## Target manuscript identity

Recommended identity:

```text
primary genre: conceptual ecology + empirical case study
supporting material: literature context / evidence boundary
avoid genre: definitive meta-analysis; evidence-gap paper; focal natural-history
             paper; universal trait evolution proof
```

## Working titles

```text
Pollination costs without resolved defence benefits: a conditional framework for
floral attraction-defence regimes

When floral defence candidates carry pollination costs: a conditional framework and
an Impatiens case study

Conditional attraction-defence regimes in flowers: theory and an Impatiens case
study
```
