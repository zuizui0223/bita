# General local sign criterion for attraction and defence

## Claim

The central theoretical result is a **local fitness-interaction criterion**, not a direct prediction of trait covariance.

For a broad fitness decomposition

\[
W(A,D)=M(A,D)-G(A,D)-C(A,D),
\]

where `M` is mutualist-mediated benefit, `G` is antagonist-mediated loss, and `C` is direct joint cost, the local attraction--defence interaction is

\[
W_{AD}=M_{AD}-G_{AD}-C_{AD}.
\]

Under the biologically oriented case used in this project:

- attraction increases exposure to floral antagonists;
- defence reduces the damage generated by that exposure;
- defence can also reduce the mutualist-mediated return to attraction;
- attraction and defence can share a direct construction or allocation cost.

The criterion can therefore be written as

\[
W_{AD}=
\underbrace{\Gamma_{AH\times DH}}_{\text{antagonist relief}}
-
\underbrace{\Gamma_{AP\times DP}}_{\text{mutualist interference}}
-
\underbrace{C_{AD}}_{\text{joint cost}}.
\]

Hence

\[
W_{AD}>0
\iff
\Gamma_{AH\times DH}
>
\Gamma_{AP\times DP}+C_{AD}.
\]

This is the sign-switch condition. It does not assume a trade-off in advance; a trade-off-like local interaction emerges only when mutualist interference and/or direct joint cost outweigh antagonist relief.

## Interpretation

- `W_AD > 0`: **local fitness complementarity**. Increasing defence raises the marginal fitness return to attraction.
- `W_AD < 0`: **local fitness substitutability**. Increasing defence lowers the marginal fitness return to attraction.
- `W_AD = 0`: local neutrality at the evaluated point.

These statements concern the local curvature of the declared fitness surface.

They do **not** by themselves imply:

- positive or negative trait covariance among populations or species;
- a genetic correlation;
- coevolution toward a particular phenotype;
- a universal ecological trade-off;
- the prevalence of either regime in nature.

Those stronger outcomes require an explicit evolutionary or comparative model in addition to the local fitness criterion.

For the implemented baseline score,

\[
W(A,D,R)=
P(b_0+b_AA)e^{-c_DD}(1-c_RR)
+(1-P)a_RR
-H(f_0+d_AA)(1-e_FD)
-C(A,D,R),
\]

with direct joint cost term `c_AD A D`,

\[
W_{AD}
=
H d_Ae_F
-
P b_Ac_De^{-c_DD}(1-c_RR)
-
c_{AD}.
\]

Thus the baseline maps onto the general decomposition as

\[
\Gamma_{AH\times DH}=Hd_Ae_F,
\]

\[
\Gamma_{AP\times DP}=Pb_Ac_De^{-c_DD}(1-c_RR),
\]

and

\[
C_{AD}=c_{AD}.
\]

The numerical robustness sweep varies curvature around this corollary. The theorem-level novelty is the decomposition and break-even inequality; the grid is a sensitivity analysis over selected functional forms, not the proof itself.

## Empirical bridge

The literature layer currently supports only a narrower statement: a negative `B -> P` pathway exists in at least some systems. That establishes the biological existence of one sign-changing pathway represented by `mutualist_interference`.

It does **not** establish that the complete mixed partial is negative in those systems, because the antagonist-relief and joint-cost terms are not jointly estimated on the same scale.
