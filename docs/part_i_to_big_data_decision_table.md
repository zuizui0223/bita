# Part I theory → big-data decision table

## Purpose

Part I derives exact and simulated conclusions for a declared fitness model. Parts
II and later must not merely collect large datasets: they determine whether
specific **predeclared empirical signatures** are compatible with, contradict,
or cannot identify those conclusions.

This table prevents a common overclaim: treating any global trait correlation as
an empirical test of the attraction--floral-barrier model.

## What Part I actually predicts

For the current score, local attraction--defence complementarity is determined
by:

```text
floral-antagonist tracking × floral-barrier efficacy
  versus
pollinator-access obstruction + shared A--D cost
```

The exact mixed partial concerns `A_flower` and `B_flower`. Leaf-consumer pressure
may change leaf investment or overall allocation, but it does not directly enter
this mixed partial.

## Evidence levels for Parts II+

| Evidence level | Required unit and data | What it can judge | What it cannot judge |
|---|---|---|---|
| **D1 Direct regime test** | Same plant population/network context; direct floral attraction traits; direct floral-barrier traits; pollination edges or service; florivory/seed-predation observations; site/time and sampling metadata | Whether a declared observation model supports, contradicts, or fails to identify the Part I `A_flower × B_flower` scenario | Causal selection unless fitness/manipulation data are added |
| **C1 Attraction component test** | Pollination edges and direct floral traits within the same study/network | Whether a floral-trait module is associated with mutualist-network structure | Floral defence, florivory, or the Part I cross-partial |
| **C2 Leaf-antagonist component test** | Explicit plant--leaf-antagonist network and direct leaf quality/resistance traits | Whether `Q_leaf`/`B_leaf` is associated with leaf-antagonist structure | The Part I floral `A × D` result without an explicit cross-organ bridge |
| **C3 Separate source feasibility result** | Trait or interaction source that passes/fails access and metadata gates | Whether a proposed data route is reproducibly available | Any biological effect or theory sign |
| **N0 Non-test** | Unmatched pollination and herbivory databases, imputed traits, or pairwise claims without sampled-network units | Nothing about the Part I sign | A trade-off, complementarity, or absence of evidence |

## Decision rules

### Direct support / contradiction / non-identifiability

A D1 analysis may be labelled:

- **compatible with scenario S** when its preregistered observation model yields
the expected direction under the sampling and phylogenetic sensitivity checks;
- **contradicts scenario S** when the opposite direction is robust and the
proxy mapping/sampling model are adequate; or
- **not identified** when trait coverage, matched interaction layers, sampling,
or mapping conditions fail.

No D1 analysis may be called proof of adaptation without a fitness or selection
component.

### How C1 and C2 contribute

C1 and C2 are useful evidence about individual links in the mechanism map. They
can narrow reasonable parameter regimes or motivate a future D1 test, but they
do not substitute for D1. In particular:

```text
leaf quality ↔ herbivory structure
≠
floral attraction × floral barrier complementarity
```

### Immediate data-source consequence

The current public-data probes are C3 feasibility tests:

1. BIEN leaf records test whether a public `Q_leaf` route can exist.
2. GloBI contract screening tests whether public antagonist claims have the
   sampled-network metadata required for a C2 route.
3. A successful C2 route remains a component test; it does not magically become
   a direct D1 test.

## Publication logic

```text
Part I: exact conditions + simulation phase map
Part II: public-data feasibility and, where justified, component analyses
Part III: matched floral network--trait--antagonist synthesis for direct regime tests
```

The project is successful at each completed level. A failed C3 route is recorded
as a reproducibility/identifiability finding, not patched by manual data filling.
