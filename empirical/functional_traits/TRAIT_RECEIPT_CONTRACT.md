# Trait receipt and coverage-audit contract

## Purpose

A trait request, an exported spreadsheet, and a model-ready trait table are not
the same object. This contract specifies the normalised receipt table required
between them. It preserves enough information to distinguish a direct trait
record from an imputed proxy and to decide, reproducibly, whether each
literature-selected functional trait is usable in the global network analysis.

## Inputs

The audit combines three tables:

1. **plant manifest** — the Web of Life plant set generated by
   `scripts/prepare_try_wol_request.py`, with either `plant_taxon` or
   `plant_taxon_as_reported` and preferably `network_names`;
2. **trait-role evidence matrix** —
   `empirical/functional_traits/trait_role_evidence.csv`; and
3. **normalised trait receipt** — one row per source record in the contract
   below.

Raw TRY or other provider exports must be retained unchanged outside this
normalised table. The normalisation step maps provider-specific column names and
trait labels to the stable `trait_id` values in the evidence matrix.

## Required normalised receipt columns

```text
plant_taxon
trait_id
trait_value
trait_unit
trait_source
trait_observation_level
imputation_flag
taxon_match_status
```

Optional but recommended provenance columns:

```text
accepted_plant_taxon
source_record_id
source_dataset_id
source_version
measurement_method
measurement_date_or_period
normalisation_note
```

### Field rules

- `plant_taxon`: spelling from the requested plant manifest after a documented
  taxonomic reconciliation; it is the join key for coverage calculation.
- `trait_id`: one of the identifiers in `trait_role_evidence.csv`, not a free
  provider label.
- `trait_value`: non-empty raw or standardised value. Do not enter an imputed
  value as though it were observed.
- `trait_unit`: preserve the provider unit. Unit conversions belong in a later,
  declared harmonisation step.
- `trait_source`: source database, dataset, publication, or provider record.
- `trait_observation_level`: `individual`, `population`, `species`,
  `infraspecific`, `genus`, or another declared level. The first coverage audit
  counts only individual/population/species/infraspecific direct records.
- `imputation_flag`: `true` or `false`. Imputed records are reported but never
  count toward direct trait coverage.
- `taxon_match_status`: `exact`, `accepted_synonym`, or `unmatched`. Only the
  first two are eligible for coverage.

## Coverage decision

For each candidate trait, the audit reports:

```text
requested plants
plants with direct records
plants with only imputed records
direct plant coverage
inclusive (direct + imputed) coverage
networks with at least 60% direct trait coverage
```

A predeclared primary trait is labelled `ready_for_primary_analysis` only if:

1. direct records cover at least 60% of requested plants; and
2. at least 30 requested networks have at least 60% direct plant coverage for
   that trait.

This is a **trait-specific readiness decision**, not a full backbone approval.
The existing interaction-network audit still determines whether network count,
region count, interaction-layer integrity, weights, and provenance meet the
broader backbone requirements.

Traits marked `hold_for_coverage_audit` in the evidence matrix remain
conditional even if coverage is high. A separate design decision is required
before they become focal predictors.

## What cannot be claimed

- High coverage does not demonstrate that a trait has the predicted biological
  function.
- Low coverage does not demonstrate that the trait has no ecological effect.
- Imputed values can support an explicitly labelled sensitivity analysis but do
  not replace direct records in the primary analysis.
- A receipt with taxonomically unmatched rows must not be silently merged by
  accepted-name string alone.
