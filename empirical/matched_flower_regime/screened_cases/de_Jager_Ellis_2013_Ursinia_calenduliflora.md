## Why it entered the targeted cohort

The OpenAlex metadata record identifies a polymorphic annual daisy and reports
three relevant components in its abstract-level description:

```text
- floral morph differences;
- pollinating and florivorous insect visitation or damage; and
- reproductive-fitness observations across populations and years.
```

This makes the article a targeted **screening lead**, not evidence that a
recoverable direct trait → pollination or trait → antagonist coefficient exists.

## Current boundary

```text
M0_candidate_needs_full_text
no study-card record
no effect registry record
no A_flower or B_flower role assignment
```

A future full-text screen must verify, from methods and tables, whether the
floral morph is a direct individual trait predictor; whether pollination and
florivory have compatible denominators; and whether a quantitative effect with
uncertainty is recoverable. Population-level visitation patterns or morph
contrasts without a direct eligible effect do not qualify for the registry.
