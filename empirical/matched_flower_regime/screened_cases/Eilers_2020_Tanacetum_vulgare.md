## Measured within the experiment

The study measured flower-head production and diameter, bloom duration, floral
headspace volatiles, and pollen nutrient/chemical variation across *T. vulgare*
chemotypes. It also measured behavioural attractiveness to florivorous *Olibrus*
beetles.

```text
floral trait candidates  → florivorous-beetle response
pollination response     → not measured
```

## Why the study is M1, not M2 or D1

No pollinator visitation, pollen-transfer, or pollination-success outcome is
measured in the experiment. Floral/pollen terpenoids are not assigned to
`B_flower` merely because they could be defensive: the study does not establish
a separate flower-specific barrier efficacy pathway.

The public full-text screen also does not yield a recoverable effect estimate
with uncertainty and a declared observation exposure/denominator that meets the
four-path registry rule. Therefore no `A_to_antagonism` effect is registered at
this stage.

## Interpretation boundary

This paper supports the existence of an experimentally tractable floral-trait /
florivore pathway. It does not test the attraction–barrier interaction, does not
supply an aligned two-channel panel, and must not be used as evidence that a
floral chemical trait has a direct defensive effect or that pollinator and
florivore responses trade off.
