# Host-evidence search plan

## Search objective

Populate a Japan-only evidence ledger of *Megachile* nesting-material records without conflating those records with floral hosts.

## Search blocks

Run every block in Japanese and English, retaining the literal query and search date in the ledger metadata.

```text
"Megachile" AND (leaf cutting OR cut leaf OR nest lining OR nest material OR resin)
"Megachile" AND (巣材 OR 葉片 OR 葉を切る OR 切葉 OR 巣内壁 OR 樹脂)
"ハキリバチ" AND (巣材 OR 葉片 OR 葉を切る OR 切葉 OR 樹脂)
[species name] AND (nest material OR leaf OR petal OR resin)
[species name] AND (巣材 OR 葉片 OR 樹脂)
```

## Screening sequence

1. Does the source concern Japan or give a locality that can be checked as Japan?
2. Does it describe nesting material, rather than pollen/nectar use or floral visitation?
3. Is the bee identity explicit at species, genus, or higher level?
4. Is the plant identity explicit at species, genus, or higher level?
5. Can the original statement be located to a page, figure, label, specimen record, or image?
6. Can the interaction be assigned a grade A--E under the protocol?

## Deliberate exclusions from the primary universe

- pollen host records;
- flower-visitor lists;
- plant damage that is visually suggestive but lacks bee identification;
- sources that say only "uses leaves" without a plant taxon;
- records outside Japan;
- garden advice pages without sourceable observation.

## First stopping rule

Do not begin the formal spatial analysis after finding a few attractive examples. Stop the first search pass only when:

```text
all named Japanese Megachile taxa in the bee checklist have been searched;
all direct nesting-material records are entered or marked not found;
plant taxonomy is reconciled for the evidence-backed candidates;
trait coverage has been audited for the primary candidate universe.
```

A sparse but transparent evidence table is preferable to a large, mixed-quality host list.
