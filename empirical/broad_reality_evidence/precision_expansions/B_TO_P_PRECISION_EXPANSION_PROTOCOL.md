# B-to-pollination precision expansion v1

## Why this separate corpus exists

The first abstract-level calibration batch contained no direct
`B_to_pollination` record. This does not mean the route is biologically absent.
It showed that broad search membership from scent, chemistry, or pollination
terms often fails to establish a **flower-specific barrier** with a direct
pollinator outcome.

This expansion therefore targets the missing empirical design:

```text
flower-specific chemical or physical barrier/access trait
        -> direct pollinator visitation, preference, or pollen-transfer outcome
```

## Relation to broad corpus v2

```text
broad_reality_evidence_v2
    high-recall all-route corpus; unchanged and separately reproducible

B_to_P_precision_expansion_v1
    targeted additional discovery universe; never silently merged into v2
```

A study found here still must pass the same source-screening and role rules.
Query membership does not establish B, P, an effect sign, or an experimental
manipulation.

## Query logic

The expansion deliberately excludes generic floral scent searches. Scent is an
A trait by default and can be called B only when the study independently
establishes a flower-specific deterrent/resistance/access role.

The eight query routes target:

```text
floral defense / defence with visitation experiments
floral chemical defense with pollinator preference
nectar alkaloids and pollinator choice
flower-specific deterrence and pollination costs
flower/corolla trichomes and visitation
floral toxins and visitation
```

## Inclusion gate after retrieval

A source is eligible for direct B-to-P route coding only when it has:

```text
1. a direct flower-specific chemical or physical barrier/access trait;
2. a stated reason that the trait is barrier/resistance/deterrence rather than
   attraction or reward alone;
3. a pollinator outcome with an identifiable unit or exposure;
4. a direction recoverable from source text, table, figure, or public data.
```

Source records that only describe floral chemistry, visitor communities, or
fitness effects remain broad context and do not enter B-to-P.

## Analysis destination

Eligible records feed exactly one of the predeclared B-to-P strata:

```text
BP_chemical_visitation_lrr_manipulation
BP_physical_visitation_lrr_manipulation
```

Observational B-to-P records are retained in the direction map but are not
pooled with manipulation estimates. If the route remains sparse after this
precision expansion, that is an evidence-gap result to report alongside Part I,
not a reason to relax the B role definition.
