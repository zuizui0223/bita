The current records cannot support a pooled B-to-P effect because the available source basis is deposited abstract text, while outcome layer, dose relation to natural nectar, and experimental-panel structure remain unresolved.

## Why the distinction matters

The four records already indicate that a single universal B-to-P cost is biologically implausible:

- the *Gelsemium* record is explicitly context-dependent;
- the bird nicotine record varies across species and sucrose context;
- the *Delphinium* record distinguishes high-dose effects from natural-range treatments;
- the *Toxicoscordion* record is a controlled generalist-foraging deterrence assay rather than a community-wide pollination measure.

These statements are prompts for source verification, not numerical conclusions. The full-text pass must verify the specific treatment levels, response units, and independent panels before any use in an effect synthesis.

## Consequence for the paper

Until a compatible numerical stratum is populated, B-to-P enters the paper as:

```text
mechanistic evidence that a flower-specific chemical barrier can impose
pollinator-use costs under particular dose, consumer, and ecological contexts
```

It does **not** enter as a pooled average effect, a generic pollination-fitness effect, or an unconditional trade-off.
