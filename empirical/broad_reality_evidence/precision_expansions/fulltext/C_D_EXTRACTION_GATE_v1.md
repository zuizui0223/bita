# c_D chemical-barrier extraction gate v1

## Why this route is next

`c_D` (flower-specific barrier -> pollinator-use cost) is the only Part B arrow
with current directional support at the B1 threshold: three independent chemical-
barrier manipulation clusters point toward reduced pollinator preference or
foraging. That is directional support, not yet a pooled effect-size estimate.

The fixed full-text queue contains four pre-registered studies:

```text
Gelsemium sempervirens, nectar alkaloid manipulation
bird nectar alkaloid / nicotine manipulation
Delphinium barbeyi, alkaloid manipulation
Toxicoscordion paniculatum, zygacine manipulation
```

## C3 gate: access before extraction

The associated resolver writes only Crossref access and relation receipts. A
resolved DOI, publisher link, licence, or linked-data relation does **not** prove
that any usable statistic exists.

## C4 extraction requirements

A study moves from the fixed reading queue to a numerical extraction sheet only if
all of the following are recorded from the source:

1. intervention and control composition, including alkaloid/chemical dose;
2. whether the relevant contrast is natural-range, within-natural-range, or
   supra-natural;
3. pollinator response definition and denominator (e.g. visits, choice trials,
   time per flower, feeding frequency);
4. experimental unit, replication, and uncertainty or recoverable raw counts;
5. source locator (table, figure, supplement, or data archive);
6. a compatibility cell that does not mix pollinator species, sucrose contexts,
   response layers, or dose regimes without an explicit multilevel model.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- Do not reinterpret foraging/preference as pollen transfer or plant fitness.
- Do not average natural-range and supra-natural doses.
- Do not pool different pollinator taxa or response units merely to reach `k = 3`.
- Do not replace the B2 threshold with directional B1 support.

The intended next result is either a small, compatible `c_D` B2 envelope or an
honest report that the source materials do not yield a compatible numerical stratum.
